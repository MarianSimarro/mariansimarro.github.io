My primary research interest lies at the intersection of language and communication, using computational and behavioural neuroscience. Currently, my work focuses on how the brain combines smaller bits of language into complex linguistic and conceptual structures. I am investigating this topic as a RA with Efthymia (Effie) Kapnoula and Drew J. McLaughlin at the Basque Center on Cognition Brain and Language. I'm also collaborating with Carly Demopoulos to study fine motor abilities of autistic individuals in late childhood through adolescence and examine their associations with expressive verbal abilities.

Outside of research, I am often busy exploring the outdoors, on foot or by bike, and taking pictures. In my spare time, I also enjoy playing clarinet, baking and a good book.
