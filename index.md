My primary research interest lies at the intersection of language and communication, using computational and behavioural neuroscience. Currently, my work focuses on whether and how interleaved activation of new words with existing knowledge aids word learning and the role of prior knowledge in this process, and the effects of familiarity in multi-talker and multi-accent switching costs. I am investigating these topics as a RA with [Efthymia (Effie) Kapnoula](http://effiekapnoula.com/index.html) and [Drew J. McLaughlin](https://sites.google.com/view/drewjmclaughlin/home?authuser=0) at the [Basque Center on Cognition Brain and Language](https://www.bcbl.eu/). I'm also collaborating with [Carly Demopoulos](https://autismneuro.ucsf.edu/) to study fine motor abilities of autistic individuals in late childhood through adolescence and examine their associations with expressive verbal abilities.

Outside of research, I am often busy exploring the outdoors, on foot or by bike, and taking pictures. In my spare time, I also enjoy playing clarinet, baking and a good book.
